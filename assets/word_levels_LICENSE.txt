word_levels.tsv is derived from the CEFR-J Vocabulary Profile (ver 1.5),
compiled by Yukio Tono, Tokyo University of Foreign Studies (CEFR-J
project, http://www.cefr-j.org/), distributed via
https://github.com/openlanguageprofiles/olp-en-cefrj

Per the dataset's terms of use: "CEFR-J vocabulary and grammar profile
datasets can be used for research and commercial purposes with no
charge, provided that you cite the dataset properly. The copyright
belongs to Tono Laboratory at TUFS (Tokyo University of Foreign
Studies)."

Reference:
The CEFR-J Wordlist Version 1.5. Compiled by Yukio Tono, Tokyo
University of Foreign Studies. http://www.cefr-j.org/download.html

Entries were filtered to words that also appear in this app's
assets/ejdict.tsv game dictionary. Only the coarse CEFR level
(A1/A2/B1/B2) is kept; this app maps it approximately to Japanese
school grade bands (elementary/JHS/HS) for convenience, which is an
approximation, not an official MEXT curriculum mapping.

The app also lets users relabel the same four CEFR-based buckets as
approximate 英検 grades, TOEIC score bands, TOEFL iBT bands, or IELTS
bands, based on each test's own published CEFR correspondence tables.
This does not reflect actual per-test word frequency (no such open
dataset exists for these tests) — it is the same underlying CEFR
classification shown under a different exam's scoring scale.
